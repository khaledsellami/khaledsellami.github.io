About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Université Laval (Canada) specializing in AI-driven software architecture and distributed systems. My research focuses on developing intelligent approaches for automated microservices decomposition, resulting in four peer-reviewed publications in leading journals and conferences including Empirical Software Engineering (EMSE) and Information and Software Technology (IST). Previously, I completed a Master's degree in Smart Systems from the National School of Computer Science (Tunisia), with a focus on deep learning applications. Fully bilingual in English and French, I combine theoretical expertise in machine learning with strong practical experience in cloud-native technologies (Kubernetes, Docker), distributed systems design, and Machine Learning frameworks (PyTorch, Transformers).

Research

While microservices architecture has proven effective for large-scale applications due to its modularity and cloud compatibility, migrating from an established monolithic system to a microservices architecture remains an expensive and challenging process. The critical decomposition step of partitioning the monolith's components into target microservices has been cited as the main obstacle.
My thesis focuses on studying automated decomposition and refactoring approaches for the purpose of modernizing the migration and modernization process of software architectures. I introduce AI-based approaches ranging from evolutionary algorithms to LLM-based representation learning to improve the efficiency of decomposition approaches and provide better microservices recommendations. In addition, this thesis introduces an approach to automatically refactor monolithic interactions into RPCs using a combination of Gen AI models and meta-modelling methods.

Research Interests: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Microservices, Code Refactoring, Cloud

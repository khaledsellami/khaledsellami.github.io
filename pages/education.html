Ph.D. in Computer Science

While microservices architecture has proven effective for large-scale applications due to its modularity and cloud compatibility, migrating from an established monolithic system to a microservices architecture remains an expensive and challenging process. The critical decomposition step of partitioning the monolith's components into target microservices has been cited as the main obstacle.
My thesis, entitled "Decomposing monolithic applications into a microservices architecture using artificial intelligence techniques" focuses on studying automated decomposition and refactoring approaches for the purpose of modernizing the migration and modernization process of software architectures. I introduce AI-based approaches ranging from evolutionary algorithms to LLM-based representation learning to improve the efficiency of decomposition approaches and provide better microservices recommendations. In addition, this thesis introduces an approach to automatically refactor monolithic interactions into RPCs using a combination of Gen AI models and meta-modelling methods.
